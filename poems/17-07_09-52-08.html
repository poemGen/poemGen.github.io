I Expected Fathers


Like the senile clay of peaces your heart entertains from east to east
pure hole rescues the branches and you sobbed in the illusion and fashioned a dying billows of black smoke.

Fashion on the pigeon holes that wait for you
passing the hated chairs, filtering the doors.
In the smallest silken kiss your horse is a quilt filled with hushed fellowship.
