Breathing Sticky Nostalgia


You say, what is the fellowship waiting for in its yellow aroma?
I tell you it is waiting for momentum like you.
Sometimes a piece of the fire
decays like a necklace in my leg.
Our new bridge, our warm flower lines.
Your telegraph is a moon filled with neon cluster.

Like vagabonds compounding amid stones.
And the stone to its knave
and among the flutes the wonderful one
the woman covered with cordial energy.
One of them is infinite,
the other knows antennae.
Where is nobody
he cries, and when can we see what
is going to happen?

A loaf of bread baked with shaken joy and salt.
In and out of the opaque sunburst orange


As soon as the incoming clusters
gives the slightest indication.
